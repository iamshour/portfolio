Project

Shour & Co
Vintage Car Showroom

Genre

Web Design

Year

2020

Org

Side Project

Situation

Another side project that I’ve spent working on my free time was designing this simple landing page, for a virtual business that shows virtual automobiles to anyone interseted in antiques and historic machines.
The simplicity of the interface was a key factor of accomplishing desired design plans in a short period of time, taken into account landing pages regular structure and it’s familiarity among users. Furthermore, the current design was exclusive for a desktop viewport, and thus not responsive for more viewports.

Preparation

Idea, Logo, Color Palette, Font & Tools used

My inspiration for this simple project was my great deal of interset in automobiles and historic machinary in general, thus I decided to design a very simple landing page for a virtual business that I called “Shour & co”. In relation to the name of the business and its main objective, I designed a simple logo consisted of a car illustration in the top center of a rounded outlined circle, the name of the business below the circle, and a simple headline “Vintage Car Showroom” right below the name.
I picked a moderately dark color Palette to work with, consisted of “Dark Charcoal” and “Smoky Black”, in addition to regular white and a bit of “Silver Chalice” which is a lighter tint of grey.
My choice for the primary font was Google’s Sans-Serif font: “poppins”, with a lighter usage of a serif font: “Georgia”.
In addition to using Unsplash as the main resource for Images.

Action

Throughout this section, I’ll elaborate on each section of the webpage, from the home section, services section, showroom section, about us section, and last but not least, find us section, which is consisted of a simple form design.

Home Section

The first section of the page is the home section, which I kept as much simple and familiar as I could. It consists of a Navigation on the top inside its own horizontal container, the logo right below the nav in the center of the screen, and social icons aligned right at the bottom of the viewport.
The Background consists of a half visible part of a car with the whole street floaded with approaching cars in the back.

Services Section

On the first scroll comes a Services section, which gives visitors a hint and a bit more knowledge about the page and what it stands for.
I included three main services: Shop, Compare, and consult. Each service is represented with an icon, a title right below it, and a short description right below the title. The background I used is the “Smoky Black” with abit of texture added as well.

Showroom Section

After the next scroll, the showroom section is displayed It’s bold title is aligned on the top, and a very brief description right below it with a lighter tint color.
I used unsplash to pull some related cool shots of vintage automobiles. Images are aligned next to each other, forming two rows and three columns.
The background of this section is just regular white to simplify the interface a little and not make it somehow noisy.

About Us Section

Throughout this section, I briefly explain to visitors more about how the creator’s are, and how this journey and idea came alive.
The content is displayed inside a box, with a background color of regular white, in the center of the viewport, which has a background-color of “Silver Chalice” in addition to a simple cool pattern.

Find Us Section

The last section is to help visitors get in contact with the business, using a very modern contact form. It asks visitors for their First and last names, email, subject, and their message. Additional info related to the business is displayed on the left such as the address, email, phone number, and opening hours. The whole content is displayed inside a white box, with a small image of a vintage car on top, and with the same background-color and pattern as the previous section.

Final Thoughts

Another side project that have helped me apply and practice my design skills, techniques, and inspiration. Although it wasn’t necassary to go through any research or survey, my background of vintage automobiles and huge admiration to go back through historic machinery, and my great interestin antique and old stuff, have helped and supported my inspiration and intuition throughout the whole project.
